What Does the Parietal Lobe Do?

The parietal lobes main function is to receieve sensory information and interpret it. It specifically does this with touch and vision. It is the region of the brain that creates perception of body awareness and constructs the spatial coordinate system (a mental map) that respresents the world around us. It also plays a role in number recognition and relationships - specifically calendars: dates and time. This lobe assigns significances to these numbers.

Textures

Sure there are some things that look, smell and taste good, but due to the texture I will not eat it. Some examples:

  • Raspberries (why do they have hairs!?)

Another example is texture and fabrics. We want things to feel nice to the touch, but often the visual aesthetic of a fabic is equally as important - quite possibly the reason decorative pillows exsist haha.
