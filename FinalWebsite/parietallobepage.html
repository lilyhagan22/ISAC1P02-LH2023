What Does the Parietal Lobe Do?

The parietal lobes main function is to receieve sensory information and interpret it. It specifically does this with vision. It is the region of the brain that creates the perception of body awareness constructs the spatial coordinate system (a mental map) to respresent the world around us. It also plays a role in number recognition and relationships - relating to calendars and time. This lobe assigns significances to these numbers.
